# 10 Cybersecurity Jobs You Can Get With Little or No Experience

One of the biggest misconceptions about cybersecurity is that every position requires years of experience.

While many advanced roles have strict requirements, several entry-level positions are designed for people who are still building their skills.

This guide covers ten cybersecurity-related jobs that beginners can realistically pursue.

## 1. SOC Analyst

SOC Analysts monitor security alerts and investigate suspicious activity.

Typical responsibilities include:

- Monitoring SIEM tools
- Reviewing alerts
- Escalating incidents
- Documenting findings

This is one of the most common entry points into cybersecurity.

## 2. Junior Security Analyst

Junior Security Analysts help security teams identify and manage risks.

They often work with:

- Security tools
- Vulnerability reports
- Incident response teams

## 3. IT Support Technician

Many cybersecurity professionals begin their careers in IT support.

The role helps build skills in:

- Operating systems
- Networking
- Troubleshooting
- User management

These skills are valuable for future security roles.

## 4. Security Operations Intern

Internships provide hands-on experience while learning from experienced professionals.

Even short internships can strengthen your resume significantly.

## 5. Vulnerability Management Analyst

These professionals help identify and track security weaknesses.

Common tasks include:

- Reviewing scan results
- Prioritizing vulnerabilities
- Tracking remediation efforts

## 6. GRC Analyst

Governance, Risk, and Compliance roles focus on policies and risk management.

This path is often less technical than other cybersecurity careers.

## 7. Security Administrator

Security Administrators help manage user permissions, security controls, and system configurations.

This role provides exposure to real-world security operations.

## 8. Identity and Access Management (IAM) Analyst

IAM Analysts control access to systems and resources.

Key responsibilities include:

- Managing accounts
- Access reviews
- Permission controls

## 9. Threat Intelligence Assistant

Some organizations hire junior analysts to assist with threat research and reporting.

This role helps develop analytical and investigative skills.

## 10. Help Desk Analyst

Although not always classified as cybersecurity, help desk roles provide strong technical foundations.

Many successful security professionals started here.

## Skills That Improve Your Chances

Regardless of the role, employers value:

- Networking knowledge
- Linux fundamentals
- Security awareness
- Problem-solving skills
- Communication skills
- Practical projects

## Certifications That May Help

Popular beginner certifications include:

- CompTIA Security+
- Google Cybersecurity Certificate
- ISC2 Certified in Cybersecurity (CC)

Certifications should complement practical learning.

## Final Thoughts

Breaking into cybersecurity does not always require years of experience.

Many professionals start in support, analyst, or internship positions before moving into specialized security roles.

Focus on building skills, creating projects, and applying consistently. The first opportunity is often the hardest one to obtain, but it becomes easier once you gain practical experience.
