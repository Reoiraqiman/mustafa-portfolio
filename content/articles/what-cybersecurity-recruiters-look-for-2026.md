# What Cybersecurity Recruiters Look for in 2026

Many candidates focus heavily on certifications but overlook what recruiters actually evaluate.

Recruiters want evidence that a candidate can perform real-world tasks and continue learning independently.

## Practical Experience

Recruiters often prefer candidates who can demonstrate hands-on skills.

Examples include:

* Home labs
* TryHackMe progress
* Hack The Box challenges
* GitHub projects
* Security write-ups

## Strong Fundamentals

Candidates should understand:

* Networking
* Linux
* Windows
* Security concepts
* Basic scripting

Strong foundations are often more valuable than advanced tools.

## Communication Skills

Cybersecurity professionals frequently write reports, communicate findings, and collaborate with teams.

Clear communication is a major advantage during hiring.

## Portfolio Quality

A portfolio can separate one candidate from hundreds of applicants.

Useful portfolio items include:

* Projects
* Documentation
* Research
* Security scripts
* Technical articles

## Certifications

Recruiters value certifications when combined with practical skills.

Popular examples include:

* Security+
* ISC2 CC
* Google Cybersecurity Certificate

## Final Thoughts

Recruiters are not looking for perfection.

They are looking for candidates who demonstrate initiative, practical skills, continuous learning, and professional communication.
